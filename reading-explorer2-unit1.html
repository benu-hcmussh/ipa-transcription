Unit 1 Vocabulary

Addiction

IPA: /əˈdɪkʃn/

Part of Speech: Noun (U or C)

Definition: the condition of being unable to stop using or doing something as a habit, especially something harmful

Example: He is now fighting his addiction to alcohol.

Advertise

IPA: /ˈædvətaɪz/

Part of Speech: Verb (I or T)

Definition: to tell the public about a product or a service in order to encourage people to buy or to use it

Example: If you want to attract customers you need to advertise.

Battle

IPA: /ˈbætl/

Part of Speech: Noun (C or U)

Definition: a fight between armies, ships or planes, especially during a war; a violent fight between groups of people

Example: It ended in a gun battle between police and drug smugglers.

Cut down (on something)

IPA:

Part of Speech: Phrasal verb

Definition: ​to reduce the size, amount or number of something

Example: The doctor told him to cut down on his drinking.

Drug

IPA: /drʌɡ/

Part of Speech: Noun (C)

Definition: ​an illegal substance that some people smoke, inject, etc. for the physical and mental effects it has

Example: He was charged with possessing drugs.

Efficient

IPA: /ɪˈfɪʃnt/

Part of Speech: Adj

Definition: doing something in a good, careful and complete way with no waste of time, money or energy

Example: We must meet members' needs in the most efficient and effective ways possible.

Facility

IPA: /fəˈsɪləti/

Part of Speech: Noun (plural)

Definition: buildings, services, equipment, etc. that are provided for a particular purpose

Example: The school is upgrading its outdoor play facilities.

Obvious

IPA: /ˈɒbviəs/

Part of Speech: Adj

Definition: easy to see or understand

Example: I know you don't like her but try not to make it so obvious.

Recommend

IPA: /ˌrekəˈmend/

Part of Speech: Verb

Definition: to suggest that someone or something would be good or suitable for a particular purpose.

Example: I highly recommend this book to anyone who loves mystery.

Store

IPA: /stɔːr/

Part of Speech: Verb

Definition: to keep something in a particular place until it is needed.

Example: You can store these files in the archive.

Continent

IPA: /ˈkɒntɪnənt/

Part of Speech: Noun (C)

Definition: one of the large land masses of the earth, such as Africa, Asia, or Europe.

Example: Africa is a continent rich in natural resources.

Crop

IPA: /krɒp/

Part of Speech: Noun (C)

Definition: a plant such as wheat, rice, or fruit that is grown by farmers and used as food.

Example: This year’s corn crop was particularly abundant.

Flavor

IPA: /ˈfleɪvər/

Part of Speech: Noun (C)

Definition: the distinctive taste of a food or drink.

Example: Chocolate flavor is popular in ice creams.

Historic

IPA: /hɪˈstɒrɪk/

Part of Speech: Adjective

Definition: having great and lasting importance.

Example: The city has many historic buildings.

Produce

IPA: /ˈprɒdjuːs/

Part of Speech: Noun (U)

Definition: food or other things that have been grown or produced on a farm to be sold.

Example: Fresh produce is sold daily at the market.

Quality

IPA: /ˈkwɒləti/

Part of Speech: Noun (U or C)

Definition: the standard of something as measured against other things of a similar kind.

Example: This company is known for its high-quality products.

Scale

IPA: /skeɪl/

Part of Speech: Noun (C)

Definition: the size or level of something, especially when large.

Example: The scale of the project is enormous.

Seed

IPA: /siːd/

Part of Speech: Noun (C)

Definition: a small, round, or oval object produced by a plant from which a new plant can grow.

Example: Farmers are planting seeds for the next harvest.

Suggest

IPA: /səˈdʒest/

Part of Speech: Verb

Definition: to offer an idea or plan for someone to consider.

Example: I suggest we leave early to avoid traffic.

Variety

IPA: /vəˈraɪəti/

Part of Speech: Noun (U or C)

Definition: a number of different types of the same thing.

Example: The store has a variety of fresh fruits and vegetables.
