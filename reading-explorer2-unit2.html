Unit 2 Vocabulary

Apparently

IPA: /əˈpærəntli/

Part of Speech: Adverb

Definition: as far as one knows or can see.

Example: Apparently, they have already left the building.

Aware

IPA: /əˈweə(r)/

Part of Speech: Adjective

Definition: having knowledge or perception of a situation or fact.

Example: She was fully aware of the challenges ahead.

Complex

IPA: /ˈkɒmpleks/

Part of Speech: Adjective

Definition: consisting of many different and connected parts.

Example: The puzzle was more complex than it first appeared.

Curiously

IPA: /ˈkjʊəriəsli/

Part of Speech: Adverb

Definition: in a way that shows eagerness to know or learn something.

Example: Curiously, he didn't seem surprised by the news.

Experience

IPA: /ɪkˈspɪəriəns/

Part of Speech: Noun (U or C)

Definition: knowledge or skill acquired by doing something over a period of time.

Example: She has years of experience in teaching.

Interact

IPA: /ˌɪntərˈækt/

Part of Speech: Verb

Definition: to communicate or work together.

Example: The teacher interacts with each student during the lesson.

Record

IPA: /ˈrekɔːd/

Part of Speech: Noun (C)

Definition: a written account or other physical evidence of an event or process.

Example: They kept a record of all their expenses.

Season

IPA: /ˈsiːzn/

Part of Speech: Noun (C)

Definition: one of the four periods of the year characterized by particular weather conditions.

Example: Autumn is my favorite season.

Single

IPA: /ˈsɪŋɡl/

Part of Speech: Adjective

Definition: only one; not one of several.

Example: He didn't miss a single detail.

Unknown

IPA: /ʌnˈnəʊn/

Part of Speech: Adjective

Definition: not known or familiar.

Example: They ventured into unknown territory.

Accurate

IPA: /ˈækjərət/

Part of Speech: Adjective

Definition: correct in all details; exact.

Example: The data provided is accurate.

Aspect

IPA: /ˈæspekt/

Part of Speech: Noun (C)

Definition: a particular part or feature of something.

Example: The proposal covers every aspect of the project.

Combination

IPA: /ˌkɒmbɪˈneɪʃən/

Part of Speech: Noun (C)

Definition: a mixture of different people or things.

Example: The meal was a combination of flavors.

Create

IPA: /kriˈeɪt/

Part of Speech: Verb

Definition: to bring something into existence.

Example: Artists create works of art.

Cultural

IPA: /ˈkʌltʃərəl/

Part of Speech: Adjective

Definition: relating to the ideas, customs, and social behavior of a society.

Example: Cultural differences can be challenging.

Evolve

IPA: /ɪˈvɒlv/

Part of Speech: Verb

Definition: to develop gradually, especially from a simple to a more complex form.

Example: Species evolve over time.

Exchange

IPA: /ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ/

Part of Speech: Noun (C or U)

Definition: the act of giving one thing and receiving another in return.

Example: They had a friendly exchange of ideas.

Human

IPA: /ˈhjuːmən/

Part of Speech: Adjective

Definition: relating to or characteristic of people.

Example: Human behavior is complex.

Impact

IPA: /ˈɪmpækt/

Part of Speech: Noun (C or U)

Definition: the effect or influence of one person, thing, or action on another.

Example: The impact of technology is vast.

Standard

IPA: /ˈstændəd/

Part of Speech: Noun (C or U)

Definition: a level of quality or attainment.

Example: They maintain high standards of service.
